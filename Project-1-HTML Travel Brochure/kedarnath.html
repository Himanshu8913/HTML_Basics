Welcome to Kedarnath Sacred Yatra

``Kedarnath, nestled in the majestic Garhwal Himalayas of Uttarakhand, India, is an ancient and revered pilgrimage destination of profound spiritual significance. Perched at an elevation of approximately 3,583 meters (11,755 feet), it is one of the Char Dham, a set of four sacred sites, and holds a special place in Hindu mythology.

This sacred town is primarily known for the revered Kedarnath Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, the patron deity of destruction and regeneration in Hinduism. The temple, constructed with massive stone slabs, stands as a testament to ancient architectural brilliance amidst breathtaking natural surroundings. Surrounded by snow-capped peaks and serene landscapes, Kedarnath provides a surreal and awe-inspiring backdrop for spiritual seekers and nature enthusiasts alike.``


Weather: Summer (5-20°C), monsoons (July-Sept), challenging; winters (-15°C), heavy snowfall, inaccessible.

ATTRACTIONS

Gandhi Sarovar

Gandhi Sarovar is a small lake holding crystal clear water. Gandhi Sarovar is also known as Chorabari Tal because it is situated on the mouth of Chorabari Bamak glacier. Gandhi Sarovar lies on the foot of Kedarnath and Kirthi Stambh peak at an astounding altitude of 3,900mts above sea level. the lake lies about 3 km from the Kedarnath temple. An easy trek from the temple leads you to the lake, which was originally known as Kanti Sarovar. In 1948, some of the ashes of Mahatma Gandhi were immersed in the lake after which it was renamed Gandhi Sarovar.

Sonprayag

Sonprayag situated at an elevation of 1829 m is famous a site where Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati were married. Flanked by nature's bounty and glorious snow capped peaks, it is also a place where River Mandakini meets River Basuki. It is a belief that devotees can achieve Baikunt Dham with the touch of water. In Sonprayag to Kedarnath route, 5 km is covered by road and the rest 16 kilometers is covered by climbing trek.It is a popular pilgrimage route, and most people travel from Gaurikund to Kedarnath on foot or by hiring a pony or helicopter service, as there is no motorable road beyond Gaurikund.

Triyuginarayan Temple

Triyuginarayan is a well known Hindu pilgrimage located in the Rudraprayag district of Uttarakhand. This picturesque village is perched at an elevation of 1,980 and offers panoramic views of snow covered mountains of the beauteous Garhwal region. The centre of attraction of this place is the TriyugiNarayan also known as the Trijugi Narayan temple dedicated to the preserver, Lord Vishnu. The architecture of this temple resembles to that of Badrinath shrine.

TRAVEL TIPS
  1. **Fitness Preparation**: Prioritize physical fitness for the challenging trek. Regular exercise and stamina-building activities are crucial.
  2. **Acclimatization**: Spend a day in nearby towns like Gaurikund to acclimate to the high altitude before beginning the trek.
  3. **Appropriate Clothing**: Pack warm clothes even during summer. Layers are essential as temperatures can fluctuate.
  4. **Rain Gear**: If visiting during monsoon, carry rainproof clothing and gear to handle potential heavy rainfall.
  5. **Comfortable Footwear**: Sturdy, comfortable trekking shoes with good grip are vital.
  6. **Essential Medications**: Carry a basic medical kit, including altitude sickness medication, pain relievers, and any personal prescriptions.
  7. **Adequate Hydration and Nutrition**: Carry water bottles and energy-rich snacks. Ensure regular hydration and balanced meals.
  8. **Poncho or Raincoat**: Essential during monsoon to protect from rain and keep dry.
  9. **Local Guides**: Hiring experienced guides can enhance safety and provide valuable insights into the terrain.
  10. **Photography Gear**: Ensure you have a reliable camera or smartphone for capturing the breathtaking landscapes.
  11. **Respect the Environment**: Maintain cleanliness and respect local customs and traditions.
  12. **Check Weather Updates**: Keep an eye on weather forecasts and plan accordingly.
  13. **Emergency Contacts**: Carry contact information for local authorities, medical facilities, and accommodations.
  14. **Altitude Sickness Awareness**: Be aware of symptoms and descend if severe symptoms occur.
  15. **Travel Insurance**: Ensure you have comprehensive travel insurance for unforeseen circumstances.
  16. **Permit and Documentation**: Verify if any permits are required beforehand.
  17. **Stay Informed**: Keep updated with local news and advisories.
  18. **Portable Charger**: Ensure you have a portable charger for your devices.
  19. **Respect Wildlife**: Maintain a respectful distance from wildlife if encountered.
  20. **Leave No Trace**: Respect the environment by carrying out all waste and leaving no impact on the natural surroundings.
